Getting Started with the Console

        

          To get started, press one of these shortcuts to open your browser's
          Developer Tools:
          
    
            
  • 
              Mac option+command+i
            
    • 
            
  • 
              Windows ctrl+shift+i or F12
            
    • 
          

          

            Click on the Console tab. This is the debugging console,
            where you can log messages and inspect data. Here is how your
            can do that:
          

          
console.log('message 1', 'message 2'); // Outputs messages
console.log(myVariable, myObject, myArray); // Inspects data

          

            See other methods of logging to your console.
          

        

      


      

        
Getting Started with Breakpoints

        

          Try this tutorial on how to use breakpoints in Chrome.
        

        

          Breakpoints in a nutshell:
          
    
            
  • Click on the Sources tab in Chrome (or the Debugger tab in
              Firefox).
    • 
            
  • In the panel on the left, click on the browser.js file to open it in your debugger.
    • 
            
  • Place breakpoints where you would like to inspect the code.
    • 
            
  • Refresh the page.
    • 
            
  • In the panel on the right, examine any current variables underneath where it says Scope.
    • 
            
  • Press the  icon to move onto the next breakpoint.
    • 
            
  • When you are done, you can clear all breakpoints by right clicking underneath where it says Breakpoints and selecting Remove all breakpoints.
